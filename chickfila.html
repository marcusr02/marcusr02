Chick-fil-A is a popular American fast-food restaurant chain specializing in chicken-based dishes. Founded in 1967 by S. Truett Cathy in Hapeville, Georgia, Chick-fil-A has grown to become one of the largest fast-food chains in the United States, known for its distinctive Southern hospitality, quality food, and commitment to customer service. The signature item on the Chick-fil-A menu is its original chicken sandwich, featuring a breaded and fried chicken breast served on a toasted buttered bun with pickle slices. In addition to its classic sandwich, Chick-fil-A offers a variety of chicken-based options, including nuggets, strips, salads, wraps, and breakfast items such as chicken biscuits and breakfast burritos.
